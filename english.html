L I S T E N I N G

1.Decide how loud you want to be when you're listening.

To determine the best way, ask yourself, "Have you just learnt English, have you just begun practicing English, or have you heard English for a long time but still find this skill difficult?" If you're new to learning, it's inevitable that you'll hear absolutely nothing at first, so start with the basics. If you've been studying English for a long time but still have trouble listening, you should try a different approach.

2.Start with simple listening tests.

You may select videos that teach fundamental communication phrases, discussions based on real-life scenarios, videos that teach basic vocabulary by topic, or simple cartoons. The speed should be slow, the pronunciation should be clear, and the vocabulary and grammatical structures should be basic. To integrate listening, speaking, and vocabulary acquisition, you should listen or watch videos on a daily basis, take notes on new terms, and utter them out loud.

3.Make a defined path.

Set a timetable and study regimen to ensure constant listening practice. Assume you spend at least an hour each day listening closely and an extra afternoon or evening learning English. Instead of sitting at your computer, fatigued and disappointed, you should alternate between viewing cartoons, easy-to-understand English-speaking Youtube channels, listening to music, and interpreting lyrics... as long as you listen to and appreciate English more every day. It takes time to enhance your English listening abilities, so don't lose up if you find yourself listening to real English and having difficulty understanding it.

4.Focused and proactive listening to English

You should choose a peaceful place where you may listen to English with concentration and initiative. When listening, you must fully immerse yourself in order to understand the major ideas and even envision the situation. You may combine listening with keyword recording as a strategy to assist you focus as much as possible on the topic of the listening passage. If you've tried but still can't comprehend more than half of what's being said, you should practice listening with more intelligible sources and continue to work on boosting your vocabulary.

R E A D I N G

1.Learn vocabulary

This is an excellent approach to read properly and in less time if you are terrified of reading. Reading magazines and novels every day to enhance your vocabulary can benefit you a lot. Reading a lot helps you get acquainted with the grammatical patterns typically employed in writing, in addition to expanding and diversifying your vocabulary. As well as giving you greater confidence while reading a text with numerous new terms you are unfamiliar with.

2.Error analysis

We often focus on practicing and reviewing more when we self-study because we feel that the more we think, the faster our abilities and talents will increase, but this is not always the case. You guys, it's vital that you read. If you just perform it without considering your flaws, you'll almost certainly make the same errors on the test without even realizing it. So, when you've finished the Test, don't just check your answers and calculate your score; look over them carefully and explain why they're inaccurate. W R I T I N G

1.Instead of writing a lot, you should write standard.

Working hard is admirable, but if you continue to write without recognizing and addressing your errors, you will become "stagnant." On the contrary, it may result in a tough-to-correct error that will grow more difficult to remedy later. After you've finished writing, read and re-read them slowly and attentively. This can help you check for spelling and grammatical issues that you might not see when you're writing.

2.Avoid sentences that are too long or too short

When you're not competent in English, employing excessively long phrases makes it very simple to make mistakes, misunderstand sections of sentences, and confuse readers. Only sentences containing two clauses, one major and one subordinate, should be used. Using too few words in your sentences causes your writing to seem hurried and interrupted.
